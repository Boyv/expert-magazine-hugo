## The Shift to GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

The introduction of Google's *Search Generative Experience* (SGE) has radically changed SERP structures. We are no longer in a war for positions (1, 2, 3); we are in a **war for citations**.

### The Pillars of GEO

To be cited by a Large Language Model (LLM) within a generated response, three factors predominate:

1.  **Semantic Structure:** Content must be segmented by clear Hn tags and directly address conversational search intents.
2.  **Factual Authority:** Using statistics and verifiable sources increases the chances of being selected as a trusted source.
3.  **Entity-Based Optimization:** It's no longer about keywords, but named entities linked together by a knowledge graph.

> "In 2026, if you are not in the generative snapshot, you only exist for a tiny fraction of the traffic."

### Strategies to Dominate SGE

* **Adopt the Reverse FAQ format:** Anticipate the user's follow-up questions.
* **Enrich Schema.org:** Provide an ultra-precise "machine-readable" version of your expertise.
* **Direct Citations:** Use short, impactful sentences that the AI can easily "quote."

## Towards Hybrid SEO

The winning strategy involves maintaining classic SEO for long-tail informational traffic while developing a GEO layer to capture immediate visibility offered by AI summaries.
