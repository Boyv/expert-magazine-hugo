## AI Produces Text, Humans Produce Trust

With the explosion of AI-generated content, Google has tightened its **E-E-A-T** criteria. The question is no longer "did AI write this?" but "is this content helpful, reliable, and based on real-world experience?".

### How to Prove "Experience" in 2026

Experience (the first 'E') is what AI, by definition, lacks. To stand out, you must inject elements that only a human can provide:

1.  **Proprietary Case Studies:** Share actual test results with original screenshots and data.
2.  **Expert Opinion:** Don't just summarize a topic; provide a strong viewpoint based on years of practice.
3.  **Original Visuals:** Prove that you have tested the product or implemented the strategy you are discussing.

> "AI content without human oversight is a commodity. Expert content is a rarity that will always be rewarded by quality algorithms."

### Strategies to Strengthen Authority

* **Identifiable Authors:** Every article should be linked to a real expert profile, including links to social media and prior publications.
* **High-Quality Sourcing:** Don't just cite other blogs; link to academic studies or official reports.
* **Regular Updates:** E-E-A-T also relies on the freshness of information. Outdated content loses its Trustworthiness.

## Conclusion

AI is a fantastic tool for structuring and accelerating writing, but it should only be the skeleton of your content. The "flesh"—composed of expertise and human nuance—is what will save your site from future algorithmic updates.
